Early Years

I was born in Pakistan in the early 90's and emigrated to the United States when I was seven years old with my mom and older brother. My dad and uncle picked us up from the airport and I remember feeling confused because it was day time when we landed and I could have sworn we were traveling for a really long time. The short car ride to our apartment in Brooklyn was awesome because I got to stick my head out of the sunroof. Over the years we got to move to different parts of the 5 Boroughs and experience city life. That experience played an integral part in my development into adulthood.

Education

If my life needed to have a label it would be traveler. After spending years in NYC I wanted a change, I picked up my life, packed it in a few suitcases, and moved 8 hours away to northern New York, the goodbye with my family was heart wrenching but important. In Potsdam, I earned my Bachelors in Science in Aeronautical Engineering.

Professional Endeavors

Today I am Test Engineer for Collins Aerospace. My most recent project involves validating the design of an air quality unit aboard Navy Ships. My work is challenging and important for the life support of sailors on board. Suffice to say I feel valued and am content with the current state of things.

Outside of work my main goal is develop myself spiritually and physically. I am actively learning how to read the Quran in arabic. I also frequent the local Big Sky gym to shed the seasonal weight and am building a solid routine doing that.
